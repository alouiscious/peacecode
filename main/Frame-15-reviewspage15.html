PENDER HARBOUR BLUES FEST

They're hot, they're new (their first show was in 2004), and
they've already been nominated for Best Band by the prestigious
Washington Blues Society's Best of the Blues Awards.
They are the Red Hot Blues Sisters, the headline act at this
year's Pender Harbour Blues Festival. The professional musicians
from Seattle have never performed in Canada before, but just two
years ago they sat in at the Yale Hotel in Vancouver with the
Cal Batchelor band (see below) a connection that now leads them
back to B.C. to perform on May 12 and 13. Guitarist Teri Anne
Wilson and vocalist Suze Sims front the group and are the
songwriters for the work of Chris Leighton on drums, Lissa
Romaglia and Scotty Harris.
Fans love them.
Our recent success and recognition is a blessing that we are very
grateful for," says lead singer Sims.
Find the Red Hot Blues Sisters at the Madeira Park Legion on
Friday and Saturday nights with tickets for $20 or $25 at the
door. Tickets are available at the Paper Mill or the Legion in
Madeira Park, Talewind Books and Coast Books.
More about the Blues Festival can be found at www.phblues.com or
by calling 604-883-2642.
--By Jan DeGrass

Source: Coast Reporter(BC, Canada)
Seattle’s Red Hot Blues Sisters display their proficiency at different blues styles on their new five-song EP, “Hot Off The Press.”
“Flood In The Basement Blues” is a country blues tune with a healthy helping of twang.

Lead singer Suze Sims bemoans the condition of her home after a flood and dealing with the landlord. “Everything floats in a basement, only the good stuff sunk/It only rained 20 minutes, but that was the first day, son.”

“Pour Your Heart Out” is a warning to someone being manipulated by greedy and power hungry people. “Folks on a witch hunt they burned you at the stake/take it from me it’s your lucky break.” Saxophone adds an interesting element to the music.

“Transactions” is a sophisticated, Chicago-style blues number, with a smooth saxophone line. Sims warns against materialism. “Wishful luck of mass consumers, driven by sadistic rumors.”

“Ocean Beaches” has soulful vocals and guitars. Sims professes a love of the beach to make an allegory about success and failure in life. “Ocean Beaches my next stop/waves to me on the rocks/ocean beaches my best friends/until the end.”

“See You” could be a tribute to a friend who has passed. “I’ve turned my face to the sun because I know I’m not the only one/the flowers in your garden smell so fine,” Sims sings. This song really allows her to display her vocal range.

Red Hot Blues Sisters play Fort Lewis at 2 p.m. July 4 and Celebration Park in Federal Way at 7 p.m. July 4. They play University Place Festival at 6 p.m. Aug. 5.
